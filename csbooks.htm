Recommended Books For Cognitive Science Courses

I have an updated version of this but not on the web!


There seem to be an artificial divide between artificail intelligence and cognitive science, when viewed from certain perspectives so look to the other book lists for more AI texts.


Boden, M.
Artificial Intelligence and Natural Man. (Second Edition).
The MIT Press, 1987.

Boden, M.
Computer Models of the Mind
The Cambridge University Press, 1988.

Churchland, P.M.
Matter and Consciousness (Revised Edition).
The MIT Press, 1990.
(Philosophy meets neural nets meets Cognition and mind)

Eysenck, M.W.
Principles of Cognitive Pyschology.
LEA, 1993.
(Concise overview of the subject)

Eysenck, W.W. and Keane, D.
Cognitive Psychology: The Students Handbook.
LEA, 1990.

Gardner, H.G.
The Minds New Science.
Basic Books, 1988.

Garnham, A. and Oakhell, J.
Thinking and Reasoning.
Blackwell Academic Press, 1994.

Johnson-Laird, P.N.
The Computer and the Mind.
Fontana Masters Press, 1988.

Newell, A.
Unified Theories of Cognition
Harvard University Press, 1990.
(Very important book in cognition)

Penrose, R.
The Emperor's New Mind.

(AI is impossible: an alternative viewpoint on AI/CogSci).

Sharples, M. et al. (1989)
Computers and Thought MIT Press.
(This book provides a very readable introduction to many of the important ideas in Cognitive Science and AI. It introduces the programming language Pop-11).

Stillings, N.A., Weisler, S.E., Chase, C.H., Feinstein, M.H., Garfield, J.L. and Rissland, E.L
Cognitive Science : An Introduction. (2 ed.)
The MIT Press, 1995.

Van Lehn, K.
Architectures For Intelligence.
LEA, 1991.
(Papers from CMU symposium on cognition and what are useful kinds of computational architectures for intelligence)

Wright, G. and Bolger, F.
Expertise and Decision Support
Plenum Press, 1992.
(What is an expert? How reliable are experts? The pyschologist's perspective on Expertise and Expert computer systems)
